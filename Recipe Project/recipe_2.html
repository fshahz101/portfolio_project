Aloo Paratha Recipe

Aloo Paratha, a North Indian staple, is a nourishing flatbread stuffed with a spiced mixture of mashed potatoes, then pan-fried to a golden crisp, offering a satisfying blend of comfort and flavor in every bite.

Ingredients

For the Dough:

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour
  • Water as needed
  • Salt to taste
  • Ghee or oil for cooking

For the Filling:

  • 2 large potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon amchur (dry mango powder) or chaat masala
  • 2 tablespoons finely chopped coriander leaves
  • 1 or 2 green chilies, finely chopped (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Dough:

    • Mix the wheat flour and salt in a large bowl.
    • Gradually add water and knead into a soft, pliable dough.
    • Cover the dough and let it rest for about 15-20 minutes.

  2. Make the Filling:

    • In a bowl, combine the mashed potatoes, red chili powder, garam masala, amchur, salt, coriander leaves, and green chilies. Mix well.

  3. Assemble the Parathas:

    • Divide the dough into equal-sized balls.
    • Roll one ball into a small circle, place a portion of the potato mixture in the center, and bring the edges together to seal the filling inside.
    • Gently roll it out again into a slightly thick, flat round paratha. Be careful not to let the filling come out.

  4. Cook the Parathas:

    • Heat a tawa or griddle over medium heat.
    • Place the rolled paratha on the tawa and cook until small bubbles appear. Flip it over.
    • Apply a little ghee or oil on both sides and cook until both sides are golden brown and fully cooked.

  5. Serve:

    • Serve hot aloo parathas with yogurt, pickle, or any chutney of your choice.

    6. Enjoy your homemade aloo parathas!
